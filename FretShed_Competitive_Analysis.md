# FretShed — Competitive Analysis
### Guitar Fretboard Training App Market · February 2026

---

## Executive Summary

The guitar fretboard training app market is more competitive than our project documentation has acknowledged. **FretShed is not "the only fretboard trainer that listens to you play."** At least five direct competitors offer real-time audio detection for fretboard note training via microphone. This document catalogs those competitors, maps their features against FretShed's, and recommends revised positioning language that is both honest and defensible.

The good news: while audio detection alone is not unique, **no competitor combines all three** of FretShed's core pillars — reliable pitch detection with environment calibration, Bayesian adaptive mastery scoring, and a dedicated fretboard-focused training experience with built-in tools (tuner, metronome, drone). That intersection is FretShed's real differentiator.

## Competitor Landscape

### Tier 1 — Direct Competitors (Fretboard-Focused + Audio Detection)

These apps share FretShed's core purpose: training fretboard note knowledge using real guitar input via microphone.

---

#### 1. Fret Pro

- **Developer:** Ryan Meisters (solo indie dev)
- **Platforms:** iOS, Android
- **Pricing:** Free (first two strings natural notes); one-time unlock for all strings/notes (price varies, ~$5–10)
- **Audio Detection:** Yes — built-in mic or audio interface
- **App Store Rating:** High (well-reviewed, ~4.5+)

**Key Features:**
- Asks you to play notes on your real guitar; listens via mic and confirms correct/incorrect
- Spaced repetition algorithm for note sequencing
- Progress tracking toward fretboard mastery
- Supports 6/7/8-string guitar, 4/5/6-string bass, mandolin, guitarlele
- Screen-free practice mode (speaks the note names aloud)
- Background noise suppression
- Virtual fretboard added recently for on-the-go practice without guitar
- String focus (drill a single string)

**Strengths vs FretShed:**
- Free tier is generous (two full strings)
- One-time purchase (no subscription friction)
- Multi-instrument support (bass, mandolin, etc.)
- Screen-free spoken-note mode is unique
- Actively updated (latest update July 2025)

**Weaknesses vs FretShed:**
- No environment calibration — users report detection issues (~every 5th–10th note misfires per reviews)
- No adaptive mastery scoring (uses spaced repetition, not Bayesian weighting)
- No built-in tuner, metronome, or drone
- No heatmap visualization of fretboard mastery
- No quiz modes or game variants (single practice loop)
- Reviews cite false triggers, stuck notes, and need to force-restart the app

#### 2. Solo — Fretboard Visualization

- **Developer:** Trio Software Ltd (Tom Quayle & David Beebee)
- **Platforms:** iOS, macOS, Android
- **Pricing:** One-time purchase ~$14.99
- **Audio Detection:** Yes — mic or audio interface
- **App Store Rating:** ~4.5 (iOS), ~4.5 (Google Play, 340 ratings)

**Key Features:**
- Four trainers: Note Trainer, Interval Trainer, Changes Trainer, Scale Trainer
- Listens to your playing and waits for correct note before advancing
- Interactive fretboard diagrams that react to what you play
- Intervallic function approach (not just note names — teaches intervals against root notes)
- 50+ scales and modes, 100+ chord progressions
- Voice leading exercises
- Duration-based practice sessions
- Supports 6-string, 7-string guitar, 4/5-string bass, 4ths tuning
- Companion video course ($$$) for structured learning path

**Strengths vs FretShed:**
- Far deeper music theory coverage (intervals, chord changes, scales, voice leading)
- Designed by world-renowned fusion guitarist Tom Quayle — strong brand credibility
- Targets intermediate-to-advanced players (serious musicians)
- One-time purchase model
- Companion course adds structured curriculum

**Weaknesses vs FretShed:**
- No adaptive learning algorithm — doesn't prioritize weak spots
- No progress heatmap or mastery tracking
- No environment calibration
- Android version significantly behind iOS (users report missing features)
- Reviews cite audio detection reliability issues ("can't hear it very well," "leaves me guessing")
- No tuner, metronome, or drone
- Not beginner-friendly — steep learning curve without the paid course
- No tap-mode fallback

#### 3. Guitar Blast — Learn Fretboard

- **Pricing:** Free (limited levels); subscription for full access
- **Audio Detection:** Yes — mic-based pitch detection

**Key Features:**

- Tap mode for practice without guitar
- 30+ virtual instruments to unlock (gamification reward)
- Staff training mode (standard notation)
- Custom game mode (design your own levels)
- Single string training, interval training, random training
- Backing tracks for jamming (rock, blues, flamenco, metal)

**Strengths vs FretShed:**
- Strong gamification (levels, scores, instrument upgrades, daily challenges)

- Staff/notation training
- Backing tracks for musical context

**Weaknesses vs FretShed:**
- No adaptive/Bayesian mastery algorithm
- No environment calibration
- No mastery heatmap or detailed progress analytics
- No tuner, metronome, or drone
- Small user base (56 reviews)

- Last significant feature update appears to be 2023
- Subscription model but unclear pricing

#### 4. Fretonomy (formerly Fret Trainer)

- **Developer:** Solo developer (StrongApps)
- **Platforms:** iOS, Android
- **Pricing:** Free (limited); subscription ~$2.99/month or yearly
- **Audio Detection:** Yes — "Instrument Mode" with real-time mic detection
- **App Store Rating:** Mixed (positive on content, complaints about subscription model and bugs)

**Key Features:**
- 30+ different training games and tools
- 9 instruments (guitar, 7-string, 8-string, bass variants, mandolin, ukulele, banjo)
- 950+ scales with scale explorer
- Chord pattern training (major, minor, diminished sevenths, etc.)
- Staff reading game
- Circle of fifths training
- Fretboard heatmap for progress tracking
- Chord progression generator with playback
- Tablature generator
- Ear training for notes, intervals, and chords
- Custom tuning support
- Multiple note notation systems (solfege, Nashville numbers, German, Japanese, etc.)

**Strengths vs FretShed:**
- Massive breadth of content (30+ games, 950+ scales, chord training)
- Multi-instrument support (9 instruments)
- Heatmap progress tracking (similar to FretShed)
- Internationalization (multiple notation systems, many languages)
- Theory depth (circle of fifths, intervals, staff reading)

**Weaknesses vs FretShed:**
- Audio detection reliability issues per reviews ("trouble recognizing certain notes," "error threshold" complaints)
- No environment calibration
- No adaptive mastery algorithm (heatmap tracks stats but doesn't drive quiz selection)
- No tuner, metronome, or drone integration
- Solo developer — bug reports sometimes slow to fix
- Subscription model generates pushback in reviews
- Breadth over depth — many games but none deeply polished

### Tier 2 — Broader Guitar Learning Apps (With Fretboard/Audio Components)

These are general guitar learning platforms. They use audio detection but fretboard note memorization is a secondary feature, not the core product.

---

#### 5. Yousician

- **Platforms:** iOS, Android, Mac, Windows
- **Pricing:** Free (limited daily plays); Premium+ ~$29.99/month or $179.99/year
- **Audio Detection:** Yes — advanced sound recognition for notes, chords, and strumming
- **Users:** 20M+ monthly
- **Focus:** General guitar learning (songs, chords, technique, not fretboard memorization)

**Relevance to FretShed:** Yousician proves the market accepts audio-detected guitar apps. However, it does not offer dedicated fretboard note memorization training. Its "Workouts" section includes scales and ear training, but these are secondary to song-based learning. Its pricing ($30/mo) is 6x FretShed's planned price.

---

#### 6. Fretello

- **Platforms:** iOS, Android, macOS
- **Pricing:** Free trial (7 days); subscription (pricing varies)
- **Audio Detection:** Yes — real-time feedback with AI analysis
- **Focus:** Structured guitar lessons, beginner-focused

**Relevance to FretShed:** Fretello uses AI and audio detection for real-time playing feedback, plus an AR "Mirror" feature that overlays finger positions on your guitar via camera. Impressive tech, but focused on lesson progression (chords, songs, technique), not fretboard note memorization specifically. Not a direct competitor to FretShed's core use case.

#### 7. Simply Guitar / Rocksmith+

- **Audio Detection:** Yes
- **Focus:** Song-based learning and general guitar instruction
- **Relevance:** These use audio detection for playing along with songs, not for fretboard note drilling. Indirect competition at best.

## Key Competitive Insights

### 1. Audio Detection Is NOT Unique

At least five fretboard-focused apps offer mic-based audio detection: Fret Pro, Solo, Guitar Blast, Fretonomy, and broader platforms like Yousician and Fretello. **The claim "the only fretboard trainer that listens to you play" is factually incorrect and should be retired immediately.** Using it risks:
- Looking uninformed to users who've tried competitors
- Potential App Store review issues if Apple considers it misleading
- Losing credibility with the partner's guitarist audience (who likely know these apps)

### 2. Audio Detection RELIABILITY Is the Real Gap

While competitors offer audio detection, **user reviews consistently cite reliability problems** across the board:
- Fret Pro: "Every 5th to 10th input" misfires, false triggers, stuck notes
- Solo: "Can't hear it very well," "leaves me guessing"
- Fretonomy: "Trouble recognizing certain notes," requests for adjustable error thresholds
- Guitar Blast: Crash issues, less feedback on detection quality

**No competitor offers environment calibration.** FretShed's calibration system (silence measurement → string test → profile persistence → pre-seeded detector) is genuinely unique in this market. This is the honest, defensible differentiator.

### 3. Adaptive Learning Is Underserved

Most fretboard trainers use random note selection or simple spaced repetition. Only Fret Pro claims spaced repetition, and no competitor uses Bayesian adaptive mastery scoring that dynamically weights quiz selection based on per-position performance data. FretShed's adaptive algorithm is a genuine technical advantage.

### 4. All-in-One Toolset Is Uncommon

Most fretboard trainers are single-purpose. FretShed bundles a tuner, metronome, and drone — tools guitarists need during practice — into the same app. Only Fretonomy offers a tuner and metronome alongside fretboard training, and theirs are described as "basic."

### 5. Theory Depth Is FretShed's Biggest Feature Gap

Solo and Fretonomy significantly outpace FretShed on music theory coverage (intervals, scales, chord changes, staff reading, circle of fifths). If FretShed expands beyond note memorization in the future, these features represent the growth path.

## Revised Positioning Recommendations

### ❌ Retire This Language
- "The only fretboard trainer that listens to you play"
- "The only app that uses audio detection for fretboard training"
- Any "only" claim related to mic/audio input

### ✅ Recommended Positioning (Honest + Defensible)

**Primary tagline options:**

1. **"The fretboard trainer that actually gets your notes right."**
   — Focuses on detection *reliability*, not detection existence. Supported by calibration system.

2. **"Calibrated to your guitar. Adaptive to your progress."**
   — Highlights the two genuine differentiators (calibration + Bayesian scoring).

3. **"The smartest way to master your fretboard."**
   — Emphasizes intelligent adaptive learning over generic quizzing.

**App Store description hook (revised):**

> Most fretboard trainers either don't listen to your guitar or get it wrong half the time. FretShed calibrates to your specific guitar, room, and input source — then adapts every session to drill the notes you actually struggle with. No more missed detections. No more wasted practice time.

**Supporting value props (for App Store bullets or paywall):**
- **Calibrated detection** — Measures your room noise and guitar signal once, then nails every note from the first strum
- **Adaptive mastery** — Bayesian scoring focuses your practice on weak spots, not notes you already know
- **Complete practice toolkit** — Tuner, metronome, and drone built in so you never leave the app
- **Works your way** — Play with your guitar via mic or audio interface, or tap when you don't have your guitar handy

**Competitive positioning statement (internal):**

> FretShed competes in a market with 5+ fretboard training apps that offer audio detection. Our advantage is NOT that we listen — it's that we listen *better* (calibration eliminates the detection failures that plague Fret Pro, Solo, and Fretonomy) and we learn *smarter* (Bayesian adaptive scoring vs. random selection or basic spaced repetition). We also bundle practice tools that competitors don't, keeping guitarists in one app for their entire practice session.

## Strategic Recommendations

### Short-Term (Before Launch)

1. **Update all marketing copy** — Remove "only" claims from CLAUDE.md, ROADMAP.md, App Store metadata drafts, and any partner-facing materials.

2. **Lead with calibration in App Store screenshots** — Show the calibration flow as a key differentiator. No competitor has anything like it.

3. **Emphasize the detection reliability story** — Pull quotes from competitor reviews about detection failures. Position FretShed as the answer to those frustrations.

4. **Consider adding "vs" comparison content** — A landing page or in-app section comparing FretShed's calibrated detection to competitors' raw mic detection could be powerful.

### Medium-Term (Post-Launch)

5. **Monitor competitor feature development** — Fret Pro and Fretonomy are actively developed by engaged solo devs. Watch for calibration-like features.

6. **Consider interval/theory expansion** — Solo and Fretonomy's theory depth is a draw for intermediate players. Adding interval training would expand FretShed's addressable market.

7. **Multi-instrument support** — Fret Pro and Fretonomy support bass, mandolin, and ukulele. If the partner's audience includes bass players, this could be valuable.

8. **Leverage the email list advantage** — None of these competitors have a built-in distribution channel of 3,500 targeted guitarists. This is FretShed's biggest go-to-market advantage regardless of feature parity.
